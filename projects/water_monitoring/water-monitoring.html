Project

Water Monitoring Breadboard Project

A breadboard prototype that monitors water level and triggers a controlled response. Focused on sensing reliability, signal conditioning, and simple, testable control logic.

Back to projects

Problem

Build a low-cost system to detect water level and respond automatically (indication + control), using breadboarded electronics and clear, measurable behavior.

Constraints

Hardware constraints

  • Breadboard-friendly parts and wiring
  • Noise + unreliable contacts (had to design for this)
  • Stable power delivery for consistent readings

Engineering goals

  • Repeatable threshold detection
  • Clear output behavior (LEDs / actuation)
  • Simple debug + test procedure

Design approach

Sensing + conditioning

  • Water-level sensing element (probe/float/etc.)
  • Signal conditioning to reduce noise
  • Threshold detection to drive outputs

Control + outputs

  • Indicator logic (LED steps / status)
  • Automated response (drain/valve trigger)
  • Fail-safe behavior (default safe state)

Final circuit schematic

Results

Replace these bullets with your real metrics once you have them.

  • Achieved stable level detection at a repeatable threshold
  • Verified output response across multiple fill/drain cycles
  • Improved reliability by addressing wiring/contact noise during iteration

Challenges/Iterations

  • How power stability and noise show up immediately in real sensor systems
  • How to design for testability on a breadboard (debug-friendly layout)
  • How to iterate quickly: change one variable, re-test, document results
